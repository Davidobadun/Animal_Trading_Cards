Impala

Impalas can jump up to three times their height and run at speeds of over 90 kilometres per hour.

  • Scientific Name: Aepyceros Melampus
  • Average Height: 70-90 centimeters
  • Average Lifespan: 10-13 years
  • Habitat: Dry grasslands or savannahs

Impalas are medium-sized antelopes that roam the savanna and light woodlands of eastern and southern Africa. Impalas have dark brown backs, medium brown flanks, and white underbellies. Males have slender, lyre-shaped horns that are 75cm long. Females are hornless and smaller than males.

Cheetah

The cheetah's unique body structure: flexible spine, semi-retractable claws, long legs and tail allow it to achieve the unbelievable top speed of 110 kilometers per hour.

  • Scientific Name: Acinonyx Jubatus
  • Average Height: 77 centimeters
  • Average Lifespan: 10-12 years
  • Habitat: Dry grasslands, Desserts or savannahs

Cheetahs have slender, long-legged bodies with blunt, semi-retractable claws. Their heads are small with high-set eyes. A cheetah's teeth are small when compared with other big cats, which accommodates their larger nasal passages that enable quick air intake.
